## How to Set Up WordPress on Free Hosting (Step-by-Step)

*Using GoogieHost as the example — the process is nearly identical on InfinityFree and AwardSpace.*

1. Go to **googiehost.com/freehosting** and click **"Proceed with Free"**
2. Create a free account with your email — no credit card required
3. Choose a free subdomain or connect your own domain
4. Wait for account activation — typically under 5 minutes
5. Log in to your **GoogieHostPanel** (DirectAdmin-based)
6. Open **WordPress Manager** or navigate to **Softaculous Apps Installer**
7. Search for **WordPress** and click **Install**
8. Fill in: site name, admin username, admin password, admin email
9. Click **Install** — WordPress installs automatically in 1–2 minutes

Your WordPress site is now live. Install a theme, set up Rank Math or Yoast SEO, and submit your sitemap to Google Search Console.

## SEO on Free WordPress Hosting: Honest Assessment

### What Works

- Installing **Rank Math SEO** or **Yoast SEO** plugin
- Writing properly structured content with H1–H6 hierarchy
- Connecting **Google Search Console** and submitting your XML sitemap
- Getting free **SSL/HTTPS** — a confirmed Google ranking signal
- Building internal links and optimizing meta titles and descriptions
- Using **Cloudflare** (available on all three providers) for basic CDN and page speed gains

### What Hurts Your SEO

- **Slow TTFB** — overcrowded free shared servers increase Time to First Byte
- **Shared IP addresses** — your site shares an IP with thousands of others, including potentially flagged sites
- **Site downtime** — Googlebot treats unreachable sites as unreliable
- **Subdomains by default** — `yoursite.awardspace.net` carries zero domain authority vs. a root domain

**Verdict:** Free hosting is adequate for learning SEO and testing content strategy. For ranking competitive keywords in 2026, a custom domain and paid hosting with dedicated resources is necessary.

For learning SEO, testing content, and beginner portfolios, free hosting works fine.
